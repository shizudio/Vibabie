Product Perena Purple
+
Perena Purple — invitation-only VIP dashboard

Perena Purple is the VIP program I conceived and led end-to-end, from PRD through engineering delivery to marketing launch. It is a product lead by a significant metric observation: 

20% of our depositors held 70% of our AUM.
Serious users deserved a more serious experience.

Purple was built around four improvements:
1. An exclusive communication channel with direct access to founders and fund researchers
2. Priority product and protocol updates
3. A personalised dashboard
4. A macro market signals paired with protocol opinion pieces.

The goal was transparency, trust, and tangible value for the people whose capital mattered most to Perena.

I personally managed relationships with over 20 depositors at the $100K+ tier through bespoke outreach that converted conversations into committed capital.

To scale the operation, I built a VIP status visualiser from our depositor database and automated the content pipeline using a Claude project that transformed each communication brief into six formats for distribution across channels.

I defined the communication tone and used an AI model to automatically polish our information in such style : a senior female fund manager in a 1-on-1 client relationship This includes: onboarding messages, weekly update, yield change alerts, and event invitations. 

20+ VIP depositors managed personally
$100K+ Minimum deposit tier
6 Content formats automated per brief

The system proved its worth under pressure. During market downturns and Solana-related exploit events, we reached Purple users immediately — clarifying how their funds were or weren't affected before uncertainty turned into withdrawal decisions. Engagement built trust. Trust reduced churn.

The long-term vision for Purple is a private banking experience built inside a DeFi protocol.

Beyond Purple

I operated as the translation layer between protocol mechanics and user experience I attended product standups to catch UX implications early.  I crafted PRDs so I could communicate effectively with engineers about what to build without overhead, and worked togetehr with designers, come up with briefs and executed together, so everyone has enough creative ownership and clear enough constraints to ship fast and considered output.

I facilitated the upgrade of the invest page, directing the product designer to transform a plain table of products into consistent cards that could hold investment thesis and product mechanics. I caught experience failures before they shipped: an invest page that needed filtering logic before it could scale, withdrawal copy that defaulted to USD* across all assets rather than adapting per-product. I also personally deployed several frontend features directly.

Brand Building an Identity from a Logo and a Flower
+

Given only a logo and a floral concept to start with, I built Perena's complete brand identity from the ground up — visual system, tone of voice, mascot character world, merch line, and all customer-facing content. It has since become one of the most well-liked brand identities in the Solana ecosystem.

My intention was for people to remember Perena as the place where they feel safe, confident, and delighted to grow their wealth. To achieve that, the brand needed to feel emotional, personal, and categorically different from everything else that is hyper-marketed in the space.

Imagine growing your money the way you wait for a flower to bloom.

I built Perena's identity at the intersection of two worlds that don't usually meet: the cold, hard mechanics of finance and the warmth of organic nature. Where other protocols use charts and grids, Perena uses wildflower meadows, vault doors hidden in gardens, cherry blossoms surrounding gold bars. Flowers became the core motif — a visual metaphor for yield, patience, and growth.

5 visuals — click to open

In terms of colour, we wanted the brand to feel light, luxurious, and whimsical. It is realistic and cinematic to evoke trust, but also surreally beautiful — a glimpse into a wonderfully wealthy future. We almost romanticised finance: it no longer needs to feel cold or scary, but like a function that welcomes abundance and happiness.

Deep Violet is our primary colour: warm enough to feel emotional, authoritative enough to feel trustworthy, and deliberately distant from the cold blues of corporate fintech.

Gold is secondary: the quiet signal of value — a ring on a hand, a keyhole on a vault door, an embossed bar, or a hint in the beige background. It never dominates.

Gradient iridescence is our accent: glitter, shimmer, holographic light. It anchors Perena in the digital world without relying on any generic web3 visual tropes.

Editorial fashion became the campaign reference point: we treated our audience as people with taste, not wallets with addresses.

The emotional experience I wanted to create: arriving somewhere beautiful. The feeling that your money is held somewhere considered, even lavish, but never ostentatious. A private garden with a locked door — and you have the key.

Design & Marketing Building a Creative System That Outlasts Any One Person
+

I built and managed a design and marketing team of four that became the creative powerhouse behind all of Perena's output: marketing and interface design, collaboration campaigns, merch design, and physical production.

View our most updated brand and marketing assets here: Figma ↗

We built the infrastructure that made the creative function durable and transferable — brand guidelines, tone of voice documentation, Perenimals character rules, Purple-specific communication templates, and a VIP relationship database that treated depositor history as relationship capital. We developed customised Midjourney prompt libraries, a ChatGPT brand context, and a Claude project for content operations. When team members transitioned, the brand didn't drift and the system kept running without interruption.

We managed partnership marketing across the Solana ecosystem, facilitating collaborations with Jupiter, LiFi Exchange, Huma Finance, Titan Exchange, Sanctum, Meteora, and multiple regional communities including Superteam.

At Solana Breakpoint 2025, Perena ran one of the most-approached booths at the conference. We gifted fresh flowers from a flower cart, sold Perenimals blindboxes, and moved fashionable merch that people lined up for.
