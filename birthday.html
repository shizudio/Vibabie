Shina's Birthday Invite

Design & Development · 2025 · shina26bday.vercel.app

A gated digital birthday invitation — only people on the list could get in.

For my birthday, I wanted the invitation itself to feel like part of the event. Instead of a group chat message or a Partiful link, I built a custom site where guests had to enter their name to access the details — if you weren't on the list, you couldn't get in.

The gate wasn't about exclusivity for its own sake. It was about making each guest feel personally invited the moment they arrived. Your name was already expected. You just had to show up.

How it works
+

Built with React and Vite, deployed on Vercel. There's no backend — the guest list lives directly in the app, and name matching happens entirely client-side. It's a single-page app with three views: a login screen, an invitation screen, and an error state.

The user flow is simple: you land on a login page with a pencil-sketch portrait and a name field. You type your name. If you're on the list, the page fades into the invitation — a video plays, event details appear. If you're not on the list, the form shakes and tells you to check your spelling. No second chances, no workarounds.

3s Delay before content reveals

A few intentional details: audio is unlocked the moment the user submits their name — that gesture satisfies the browser's autoplay policy, so the video plays with sound on mobile without any extra tap. The volume fades in over 2 seconds rather than starting at full. Content appears after a 3-second delay so the video always gets a moment before text competes for attention.
